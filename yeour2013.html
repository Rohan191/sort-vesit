Shiksha At Yeour

Keeping in mind the theme of the events for the even semester that is Project-SHIKSHA , the Social Responsibility Team (SoRT) of Vivekanand Education Society Institute of Technology came up with an event of conducting teaching sessions for the students of 8th,9th and 10th standard. These teaching sessions were held in Graminvikas Mandal Pandit Prathamik School at Yeour. SoRT had 10 teaching sessions for the children beginning in the month of January 2014 and continuing till the end of February 2014. Teaching at Yeour was the first event of the even semester. The event started on a promising note on 11th January, 2014. Initially, the SRIs interacted with a few students and discussed their problems and what all difficulties they faced. Building a rapport and some bond with the students was the priority so as to ensure that the students could study in a friendly atmosphere. The SRIs conducted a fun teaching session on the first day which basically included interacting with the students. Before the actual inception of the event, we had talks with the Principal of the school Mrs Nutan Joshi who gave us a grass root idea of the situation of the students there and their level. We also interacted with Mr Pankaj Gurav, the CEO of a NGO called School Without Walls, who gave us ideas on how to go about the event. The NGO has been active in the school for past few years. The main motive of this event was to create awareness about the importance of education amongst the students. The main subjects taught were Mathematics and English as the students faced some difficulties in these subjects. The SRIs taught right from the basics which gave the students a clear overview of the subject. SRIs also cleared their doubts before their exams and also taught them paper presentation techniques and easier methods of studying and quick revision techniques. Overall the sessions were a good blend of fun and studies. This not only helped the students prepare for their examinations but also taught them the importance of education to equip them to be decent and productive citizens. The sessions often involved encouraging the students to opt for college education and reduce the dropout rate in the village.
